Homework part one: Creative dataviz critique

Instructions: Choose one of the following creative dataviz stories, and carefully consume it, both text and creative elements. Make sure you spend time fully with each element. Then, critique it. Below the instructions, you should write the name of the project inside an h3 tag, and write at least three paragraphs and insert them below inside three separate p tags. Don't forget to close your tags!

Choose from one of these projects:

  • Data breach hacks, Information Is Beautiful
  • Hip hop vocabulary, The Pudding
  • Drones dropped in Pakistan, Pitch Interactive
  • COVID deaths through sound, BBC

Answer at least the following questions, and be thorough and detailed in your responses: Where is the data from in this project, and what dataviz/graphics/visual elements are used? What do you think is the key message of this dataviz project? Describe the different ways in which this dataviz project is effective at conveying its key message. Mona Chalabi talks about her dataviz projects being visceral, or having to do with deep, internal feelings rather than the intellect, in order to be memorable -- in what ways do you feel this dataviz project was visceral?

Response to COVID deaths through sound:

I had mixed feelings about the data viz used in this project. On the one hand, I thought it was a really creative attempt by them to use something other than simple charts that trend up and down. Using flowers as a reprsentation of the COVID cases and deaths was compelling, but at the same time it was still a little bit confusing. Maybe that has to do with the fact that I've never really seen data displayed like that, but even after reading the explanations for what the stem and flower petals meant, I was still pretty confused. It was tough to take into account the petals rotating each day, the petals growing, and the music playing all at the same time.

Also, the music felt pretty unneccesary, not only is it inaccessible for hearing-impaired people, but that was another instance in which I didn't really know what effect they were going for despite the explanation about bell tolls and plucking.

All in all, I think it was both brave and creative of BBC to try and display the data in a unique way like flowers, I just think they could've done a better job either displaying the data or explaining it to someone who's never seen data like that.
